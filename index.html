The Route

A 47 km paddling loop through 12 lakes and 13 portages in the old-growth temperate rainforest northwest of Campbell River. No permits required, first-come campsites, and wildlife around every corner.

47 km Total Circuit
39 km Paddling
8 km Portaging
13 Portages
3 days On the Water
Free No Permits Needed

🌲 Landscape

Mirror-calm lakes, temperate rainforest, old-growth pockets, river sections, and mountain backdrops. Wildlife includes beavers, bald eagles, elk, deer, and black bears.

⛅ Late May Weather

Daytime highs 15–17°C, overnight lows 7–9°C. Expect rain showers — usually short-lived. 15+ hours of daylight. Wind can build on large lakes by afternoon.

🏕️ Camping

20+ free backcountry campsites. First-come, no reservations. Highlights: Amor Lake island, Fry Narrows, Mohun Island (old-growth). A few road-access sites charge fees.

Trip Schedule

May 28

Arrival Day — Vancouver Island

Travel to Vancouver Island and settle into accommodation near Campbell River or Sayward. Rest up and do a final gear check.

  • Check in to hotel / lodge (see Getting There for suggestions)
  • Top up on food and supplies at Campbell River grocery stores
  • Review the route map together
  • Early dinner and early bedtime
Travel Day
May 29

Day 1 — Launch & Paddle to Amor Lake

Start: Morton Lake Provincial Park boat launch on Mohun Lake · 10:00 AM

  • Paddle Mohun Lake south (~8 km)
  • Portage down to Twin Lake (0.8 km, steep descent)
  • Short paddle through Twin Lake and beaver pond
  • Portage to Amor Lake (~1.6 km)
  • Camp: Amor Lake — Mr. Canoehead campsite (tent platforms, sandy beach, boat-access only)
~14 km · 3–4 portages
Paddling Day
May 30

Day 2 — The Big Day: Brewster & Whymper

The most demanding day — includes the longest portage and two river sections.

  • Portage Amor → Surprise Lake
  • Longest portage: 2.2 km Surprise → Brewster Lake (flat after initial hill, follows logging road)
  • Paddle Brewster Lake (large, wind-exposed — start early)
  • Navigate Whymper Lake and Whymper Creek (two river sections, 300 m + 400 m — scout first)
  • Camp: Fry Lake — Orchard Meadow Rec Site or Gray Lake (Brittany Bay)
~12–15 km · includes 2.2 km portage
Hardest Day
May 31

Day 3 — Lower Campbell & Home

Paddle the final lakes and return to the launch point.

  • Paddle Gray Lake → Goose Lake → Lower Campbell Lake (paddle early — afternoon wind builds fast)
  • Portage 1.1 km uphill to Gosling Lake
  • Paddle Gosling → Higgins Lake
  • Portage 0.4 km Higgins → Lawier Lake (steep, roots — NO canoe cart here)
  • Final portage back to Mohun Lake
  • Take out: Morton Lake Provincial Park
~14–16 km · 4–5 portages

Gear List

🛶 Paddling

  • Canoe (rentals in Campbell River)
  • Paddles + 1 spare per canoe
  • PFDs for everyone
  • Canoe cart (wide/thick wheels — narrow ones struggle on roots)
  • Dry bags — waterproof everything
  • Bilge pump and sponge

🥾 Portaging

  • Pack gear into backpacks for walking portages
  • Water shoes with toe protection & grip (mandatory for Whymper Creek)
  • Tumpline or yoke for carrying canoe
  • Small portable saw (for downed trees on trail)

⛺ Camping

  • Tent with rain fly
  • Lightweight tarp (Vancouver Island rain)
  • Sleeping bag rated to 5°C or lower
  • Camp stove + fuel
  • Bear hang rope or bear canister / Ursack
  • Toilet paper + trowel

🧭 Navigation & Safety

  • Backroad Mapbooks Sayward Forest map (or Avenza digital)
  • 1:50,000 topo maps (92K3 + 92K4)
  • Compass
  • First aid kit
  • Whistle and signal mirror
  • Headlamp + spare batteries

🥘 Food & Water

  • 3 days of food (don't overpack)
  • Water filter or purification tablets
  • Bear-proof food storage
  • Camp cooking kit (pot, utensils, mug)

👕 Clothing (Late May)

  • Rain jacket and rain pants
  • Warm fleece mid-layer
  • Sun hat + sunscreen
  • Gloves for cold mornings
  • Quick-dry pants and base layers
Canoe rentals: Several outfitters in Campbell River area offer canoe and gear rentals. Book ahead for late May — season gets busy.

Getting There

Launch Point

Morton Lake Provincial Park
Mohun Lake boat launch, ~27 km northwest of Campbell River

  1. Drive north on Hwy 19 from Campbell River
  2. ~10–14 km past Campbellton, turn left onto Menzies Main (gravel logging road)
  3. Follow Menzies Main for ~12 km
  4. Turn right onto Morton Lake Road, follow 7 km to park
  5. Drive through park to the boat launch — look for Sayward Forest Canoe Circuit sign

⚠️ Active logging road — headlights on always, yield to logging trucks.

From Victoria

Hwy 1 north through Nanaimo → Hwy 19 north to Campbell River (~264 km, 3.5 hrs). Or take BC Ferries Swartz Bay → Departure Bay, then drive north. Total: ~4.5 hrs.

Where to Stay — May 28

Book accommodation near the launch the night before. Options in order of proximity:

Sayward (closest — 15–20 min to launch)
  • Lazy Acres Farm and Suites — units with kitchenette
  • Sayward Lodge (TrophyWest) — 68-acre hunting/fishing lodge
Campbell River (~30–45 min from launch)
  • Painter's Lodge — iconic waterfront lodge, premium pick
  • Anchor Inn & Suites (Best Western) — reliable mid-range
  • Comfort Inn & Suites — standard chain
  • Big Rock Motel — budget-friendly, waterfront

📅 Book early — May–September fills up fast in Campbell River.

Safety & Tips

💨 Wind on Large Lakes

Lower Campbell, Mohun, and Brewster can develop dangerous waves quickly. Paddle these early morning before afternoon winds. Hug the shoreline — never cut across open water in uncertain conditions.

🌊 Whymper Creek

Two mandatory river sections. Scout before paddling. Late May snowmelt = higher flow. Always line the canoe when in doubt. Watch for logjams at bridges.

🐻 Bears & Wildlife

No bear caches at campsites — hang all food or use a bear canister. Mice are active at night too. Make noise on portage trails.

🗺️ Leave a Trip Plan

Leave your route, expected return date, and emergency contacts with someone at home. Cell service is essentially zero on the circuit.

💧 Water Treatment

Treat all lake and creek water — filter or purification tablets. Do not drink untreated water.

🪵 Higgins → Lawier Portage

This portage (0.4 km) is steep with roots and rocks. No canoe cart — carry gear and canoe manually. Allow extra time.

🚛 Logging Roads

Active logging may occur on weekdays. Drive with headlights on at all times. Yield to all industrial traffic. Conditions can change — check locally.

☀️ Sun on Open Water

Late May means 15+ hours of daylight. UV reflects off the water — wear sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat even on cloudy days.

Emergency: Nearest hospital is Campbell River Hospital, 375 2nd Avenue, Campbell River, BC · (250) 286-7100. BC Emergency: 911. BC Parks: (250) 286-9312.
